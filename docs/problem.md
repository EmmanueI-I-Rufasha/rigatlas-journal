# The Problem RigAtlas Is Solving

RigAtlas exists to address a specific set of problems observed in offshore and industrial environments not by replacing existing enterprise systems, but by making complex systems easier to understand.

The offshore industry already uses a wide range of software platforms for asset management, maintenance, and operations. However, the presence of software does not mean the underlying problems are solved well.

RigAtlas focuses on **clarity, visibility, and understanding**, rather than ownership of data.

## ðŸ§© Asset Visibility Is Fragmented

On an offshore rig, information about equipment, systems, and infrastructure is rarely centralized in a way that engineers actually trust.

In practice:
- Asset data is split across multiple systems
- Diagrams often exist as outdated PDFs
- Maintenance history lives separately from system topology
- Engineers frequently cross check multiple sources to understand one asset

The result is uncertainty:
- What is installed?
- What is connected to what?
- Which representation is correct?

RigAtlas is designed to provide a **rig centric view** that helps engineers understand assets in context, rather than as disconnected records.

---

## ðŸ§  Tribal Knowledge Is Bleeding Out

A significant amount of operational knowledge in offshore environments lives outside formal systems.

This knowledge often exists as:
- Personal experience
- Informal notes
- Conversations
- Workarounds learned over time

As senior engineers retire and contractors rotate, this context is lost. New personnel are left with documentation that explains *what* a system is, but not *how it behaves in reality* or *what to be careful of*.

RigAtlas is intended to support the capture and association of this contextual knowledge alongside technical structures preserving understanding, not just specifications.

---

## ðŸ‘ï¸ Visualization Is Poor or Non Existent

Most existing tools present information in tables, forms, or static documents.

However, engineers tend to think spatially and relationally:
- Where is this asset?
- What does it connect to?
- What depends on it?
- What breaks if it changes?

Without clear visual representations, understanding system level impact becomes slow and error prone.

RigAtlas focuses on **visualizing relationships** between assets, networks, and systems so that engineers can reason about complexity more intuitively.

RigAtlas is **not** intended to replace:
- Enterprise Asset Management systems
- Maintenance planning tools
- Vendor specific platforms

Instead, it acts as a **clarity layer**:

The goal is not to store everything, but to make sense of what already exists.
